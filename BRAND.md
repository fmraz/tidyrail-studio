# Tidyrail Studio Brand

## Brand Promise

Useful software for everyday loose ends.

## Tone of Voice

- Clear, calm, and specific.
- Practical rather than clever.
- Confident without hype.
- Honest about local-first privacy and limitations.
- Short sentences for UI and support copy.

## Messaging Pillars

- **Local-first:** tools should work on the user's device without unnecessary accounts.
- **Low friction:** small utilities should be easy to understand and start using.
- **Exportable:** users should be able to take their data with them.
- **Focused:** each product should do one job well.
- **Respectful:** no dark patterns, fake urgency, or inflated claims.

## Visual Direction

- Clean white and soft gray interfaces.
- Charcoal text, muted teal accents, small amber/red status colors.
- Compact but readable dashboards.
- 8px radius or less for cards, panels, and controls.
- Avoid decorative gradients, purple-blue SaaS styling, beige lifestyle palettes, and glossy AI visuals.

## Product Naming Rules

- Names should explain the job or feel connected to a concrete workflow.
- Avoid platform names, trademarks, celebrity references, and misleading claims.
- Avoid names that imply regulated security, legal, medical, tax, or financial guarantees.
